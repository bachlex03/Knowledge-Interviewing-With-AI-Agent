# Waterfall Methodology

## Scope

This topic covers the Waterfall software development methodology as a linear, phase-driven delivery model.

## Focus Areas

- Definition and core characteristics of Waterfall
- Typical phases: requirements, design, implementation, testing, deployment, maintenance
- Strengths, weaknesses, and delivery trade-offs
- Fit-for-use scenarios such as regulated or stable-scope projects
- Risks, failure modes, and common management mistakes

## Notes

- Prefer interview answers that clearly distinguish Waterfall from iterative and Agile approaches.
- Emphasize sequence, documentation, approval gates, and cost of late change.
