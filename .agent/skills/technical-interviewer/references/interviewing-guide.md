# Technical Interviewing Guide

This guide provides strategies and patterns for conducting high-quality technical interviews.

## 1. Foundational Questions (Level: Junior/Mid)
Focus on the 5W1H (Who, What, Where, When, Why, How) of the topic.
- **Example (OOP)**: "What is polymorphism and why is it useful in a large-scale application?"
- **Example (Kubernetes)**: "What is the difference between a Pod and a Deployment?"

## 2. Advanced Questions (Level: Senior/Lead)
Focus on tradeoffs, optimizations, and internal mechanics.
- **Example (Performance)**: "How would you optimize a database query that is currently the bottleneck of your system?"
- **Example (Design)**: "Compare and contrast Monolithic vs. Microservices architecture for a startup with 5 developers."

## 3. Evaluation Criteria
When evaluating an answer, look for:
- **Clarity**: Is the explanation easy to follow?
- **Depth**: Does the candidate understand the "why" or just the "what"?
- **Practicality**: Do they give real-world examples?
- **Growth Mindset**: If they don't know, how do they approach finding out?

## 4. Bilingual Response Pattern
Always ensure the translation is accurate and maintains the professional tone.

### English Example:
"That's a solid explanation of Encapsulation. You correctly identified that it helps in data hiding and reducing coupling. Can you explain how you would implement Encapsulation in a language that doesn't have private access modifiers?"

### Vietnamese Translation:
"Đó là một lời giải thích rất chắc chắn về tính Đóng gói (Encapsulation). Bạn đã xác định đúng rằng nó giúp ẩn dữ liệu và giảm sự phụ thuộc (coupling). Bạn có thể giải thích cách bạn sẽ triển khai tính Đóng gói trong một ngôn ngữ không có các công cụ sửa đổi quyền truy cập (access modifiers) như 'private' không?"
